--- layout: post title: The Stress Energy Tensor date: '2012-01-27T12:08:00.000-08:00' author: Robert Speare tags: modified_time: '2017-01-25T20:42:19.583-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-3410852316732630293.post-1955417418493265187 blogger_orig_url: http://rspeare.blogspot.com/2012/01/stress-energy-tensor.html --- So far this semester, my arch nemesis. I could never understand some of the developments and justifications for this nasty thing. The stress energy Tensor is an N by N matrix which is supposed to encapsulate Energy Density, momentum flow, Energy Flux and strain. Obviously, many books do a not-so great job at developing this matrix and its ideas but I've finally had an insight - thanks to 'idiot's guide to relativity'.

1) The Stress Energy Tensor of a perfect fluid or dust (fluid at rest) is the following:
rho 0 0 0
T_ab = 0 p 0 0
0 0 p 0
0 0 0 p
This is a symmetric tensor, and, it can be proven that a symmetric tensor in one basis (coordinate system) is symmetric in another. Therefore, one can create a more general expression for the stress energy tensor that simply follows 2 rules :
a) it's symmetric in all relatively moving or even accelerated frames
b) We have a given equation of state relating Energy Density rho to Pressure P, which, can be accounted for by the offsetting of some constant or function of rho.
For example, we can create the Stress Energy Tensor out of a Tensor product between two identical 4-velocities (which will be symmetric as long as they are identical), and create some sort of offset on the diagonal to distinguish Energy density rho and Pressure P. This offset can be accomplished by the Minkowski Matrix eta, which is eta_ab = {-1, 0,0,0}:

T_ab = A v_a * v_b + B eta_ab

Where A and B are constants. The easiest approach is to construct the Stress Energy Tensor in the perfect fluid's comoving frame (where 4-velocity = {1,0,0,0}). For the 0, 0th entry we need rho, so we have:
A v_0 * v_0 + B (-1) = T_00
The zeroth component of the 4-velocity is (1), so we have
rho = A - B
Next, we look at the Diagonal entries T_11, T_22 and T_33 which must all equal pressure P.
T_11 = A v_1 * v_1 + B eta_11
Now eta_11 = 1, and the 4-velocity components 1,2,3 are all 0. (Since we are in the fluids' comoving frame. So we've got:
P = -B
solving for A, we have
A = rho + B ... A = rho - P

So we can now create the general expression for the Stress Energy Tensor in ANY frame,
T_ab = (rho - P) v_a * v_b - (P) eta_ab

Woot Woot!
