Global Balance:
Irreducibility & Aperiodicity: Our Transfer matrix has a single, unique eigenvalue of modulus one.

\begin{eqnarray}
\lambda = 1
\end{eqnarray}

If there is another eigenvalue -- possibly complex -- of modulus one, the markov chain does not converge to a steady state. This can be represented with a two block array, where a ball MUST move between two blocks.  If we introduct a small probability

"We must avoid recurring states"

Take the eigenvector of the transfer matrix, subtract it from our current state, and take the absolute value. This will be the measure of our distance from equilibrium.
