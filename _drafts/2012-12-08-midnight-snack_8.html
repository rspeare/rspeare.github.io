He ate those gobs of chocolate like his mother's heart. Below, he felt rumblings of hot fudge and Kit Kats, a chef-miked brownie and 'world class' ice cream; they were fighting for thermodynamic equilibrium, churning into a state of maximum entropy -- does balance mean coming apart?

Stranded pedestrians looked up into the night sky as they waited for red glowing hands to turn into to green glowing prammers like themselves. All was obfuscated by electric glimmer and a hot smog. A bucket of soapy water tumbled along the sidewalk, bubbles seething and sloshing over the sides to make their own mark upon the world; the revolting basin was directed by a blue-suited, practically prostrate man. He was dark-skinned and exhausted. Students walked by holding hands, reveling in their youth and unfulfilled destiny. Black abayas swam around corners, moving like wraiths in the night.

What prompted this 'a la me' moment? What caused him to sweat through a pair of boxers each time he closed his eyes into insular oblivion? Home, the other half. That terrible mix of deep contact and deeper mistrust.
